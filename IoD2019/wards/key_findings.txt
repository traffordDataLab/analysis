KEY FINDINGS:

Altrincham
<ul>
    <li>Altrincham ranks 16<sup>th</sup> out of 21 wards in Trafford where 1 is the most deprived and 21 is the least deprived on the Index of Multiple Deprivation 2019.</li>
    <li>Trafford 025A is the most deprived LSOA within the ward on the 2019 IMD and is within the 50% most deprived nationally. The least deprived LSOA in Altrincham ward is Trafford 025D which is within the 10% least deprived LSOAs in the country.</li>
    <li>Altrincham's most deprived (Trafford 025A) and least deprived (Trafford 025D) LSOAs lie side-by-side.</li>
    <li>Trafford 025A has a much higher level of deprivation relative to the other LSOAs within the ward on the Income, Employment and Education domains as well as on the overall Index of Multiple Deprivation.</li>
    <li>Due to alignment issues between LSOAs and wards (see explanation below) the area to the East of the metrolink line is not included in this report. See Timperley and Hale Central reports for data regarding this area.</li>
</ul>

Ashton upon Mersey
<ul>
    <li>Ashton upon Mersey ranks 14<sup>th</sup> (with Broadheath) out of 21 wards in Trafford where 1 is the most deprived and 21 is the least deprived on the Index of Multiple Deprivation 2019.</li>
    <li>Income deprivation affecting older people is more varied amongst the 7 LSOAs than for children, with an LSOA represented in each of the deciles from 4 (40% most deprived nationally) to 9 (20% least deprived nationally) inclusive.</li>
    <li>Trafford 012B has 18.7% of older people affected by income deprivation compared to 3.6% of children.</li>
    <li>The LSOA which includes Ashton upon Mersey village, Trafford 013C, is affected by crime deprivation to a greater degree than the other areas.</li>
</ul>

Bowdon
<ul>
    <li>Bowdon ranks 19<sup>th</sup> out of 21 wards in Trafford where 1 is the most deprived and 21 is the least deprived on the Index of Multiple Deprivation 2019.</li>
    <li>Trafford 027C and Trafford 027D within the ward are the least deprived LSOAs in Trafford for the overall measure and are ranked 32,296 and 32,534 respectively out of 32,844 nationally.</li>
    <li>Trafford 025E is affected by deprivation to a greater extent than the other LSOAs in the ward on the Crime and the Living Environment domains. It is within the 30% and 40% respectively most deprived areas nationally.</li>
    <li>Trafford 027E is affected by deprivation to a greater extent than the other LSOAs on the Income and Employment domains.</li>
    <li>10.6% of children and 10.4% of adults aged 60 and over are living within income deprived households within LSOA Trafford 027E. This ranks the LSOA within the 50% least deprived nationally for both the Income Deprivation Affecting Children Index (IDACI) and Income Deprivation Affecting Older People Index (IDAOPI) sub-domains of Income. In contrast all other LSOAs within the ward are ranked in the 10% least deprived on IDACI and in the 20% least deprived on IDAOPI.</li>
    <li>The Barriers to Housing and Services domain shows the greatest disparity across the 5 LSOAs. Trafford 025E is the lowest in Trafford overall and within the 10% most deprived LSOAs nationally whereas Trafford 027D is within the 10% least deprived nationally.</li>
</ul>
